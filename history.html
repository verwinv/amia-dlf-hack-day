AMIA hosted its first ever hack day on November 6, 2013 in Richmond, VA. The event was a unique opportunity for practitioners and managers of digital audiovisual collections to join with developers and engineers for an intense day of collaboration to develop solutions for digital audiovisual preservation and access. Discussions between managers of audiovisual collections and solutions developers provided a fruitful starting point for a hack day project ideas, including:

  1. Simple fixity tools to use when transferring files from one storage medium to another
  2. Technical metadata extraction and making use of these reports (MediaInfo, ffprobe)
  3. Simple cataloging tools for AV, with eye towards contemporary frameworks/schema
  4. Discovery tools/UX for audiovisual collections, access at scale
